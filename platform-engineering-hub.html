Platform Engineering Hub

Build internal developer platforms that make your engineering teams faster and happier.

Platform engineering is one of the fastest-growing disciplines in software. It takes the lessons of DevOps and operationalizes them by building internal platforms that make the right thing the easy thing for every developer on the team.

Key Topics

  • Internal Developer Platforms. Design and build platforms that abstract infrastructure complexity and provide self-service capabilities to developers.
  • Golden Paths. Create opinionated, well-supported paths for common workflows, new service creation, deployments, database provisioning.
  • Developer Experience. Measure and improve how developers interact with your platform. Fast feedback loops, clear documentation, low friction.
  • Platform Tooling. Backstage, Crossplane, Kratix, and other tools for building developer portals and infrastructure abstractions.
  • Team Topologies. Organize platform teams effectively. Understand the platform-as-a-product mindset and how to staff for it.
  • Measuring Success. Track adoption rates, developer satisfaction, lead time reduction, and cognitive load to prove platform value.

Getting Started

Platform engineering is a journey. Here is a practical starting path:

  1. Understand the problem. Talk to your developers. Where do they lose time? What is confusing? What requires a ticket to another team?
  2. Start with one golden path. Pick your most common workflow (e.g., deploying a new service) and build a streamlined, self-service path for it.
  3. Treat your platform as a product. Your developers are your users. Gather feedback, iterate, and measure adoption, not just availability.
  4. Build incrementally. Do not try to build an entire platform at once. Ship small, useful capabilities and grow from there.

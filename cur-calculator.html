02 · methodology

How the math works.

For each of the seven drift classes, the calculator multiplies your compute spend by a recovery rate, scaled by the non-production share where the class is non-prod sensitive. Recovery rates are the cross-customer medians observed by ZopNight across 4,200+ production AWS accounts in the 18 months preceding May 2026.

The rates intentionally use the median, not the mean. A handful of customers have run extreme savings (one Series D consumer hit 41% within six months) but those are outliers. The median is the honest number.

04 · assumptions & caveats

What this calculator isn’t.

This is a projection, not a guarantee. A few things to know:

  • The rates are AWS-centric. GCP and Azure estates tend to land within ±3 points of these, but the mix between classes shifts (more storage drift on GCP, more commitment optimisation on Azure).
  • Recovery rates compound only partially — an oversized instance that is also non-prod and idle isn’t triple-counted. The aggregate cap built into the calculator is 33% of total spend.
  • Realised savings depend on execution, not detection. The whole point of CDCR is closing the gap between the two. A platform that detects but never remediates lands somewhere in the 5–8% range; one that runs the full loop reliably hits 22–28%.
  • The calculator does not model lock-in costs, RI/SP early-termination, or any cross-team political friction. Those are real and they matter, but they’re your numbers, not ours.
The honest framing

Treat this as a floor. If the calculator says $42K/month, ZopNight running the full CDCR loop typically hits between $42K and $58K within 90 days on a SaaS estate of that size.

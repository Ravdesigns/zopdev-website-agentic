Statement · scroll lab

Four scroll animations.
One copy. Pick the one.

The homepage statement — "Ship faster. Spend smarter. Stay audit-ready." — currently auto-reveals on a timer once the section pins. This lab rebuilds it four different ways, all tied to scroll position instead of a timer. Scroll through each variant to feel how it reads, then call which one to ship (or hybrid the ones you like).

Shared rules across all four variants:
· Same copy — "Ship faster. / Spend smarter. / Stay audit-ready."
· Same type system — Inter, ink color, tight letter-spacing
· Section pins for 100vh, scroll budget varies (1.0–1.8 extra vh)
· All driven by raw scroll progress — no autoplay timers, no jank
01 · Scrub-linked sequential reveal

Three lines, each tied to a third of the scroll.

Section pins for the full duration. Line 1 reveals at 15% scroll progress, line 2 at 40%, line 3 at 65%. Scrub back up and they unwind. The feel is "controlled and cinematic" — user dictates the pace.

Ship faster. Spend smarter. Stay audit-ready.
02 · Word-by-word kinetic type

Each word lands on its own beat.

Every word reveals independently as the user scrolls. Subtle scale + lift on each word, the verbs ("Ship", "Spend", "Stay") flash orange briefly to anchor each statement. Most cinematic, takes the most scroll budget. Heaviest motion.

Shipfaster. Spendsmarter. Stayaudit-ready.
03 · Single morphing line

One line at a time, scrolled through.

Same center spot, three states. Scroll cross-fades between them — "Ship faster" lifts out as "Spend smarter" lifts in. Tighter focus, shorter pin (50% less scroll budget). Risk: feels less weighty than three lines together.

Ship faster. Spend smarter. Stay audit-ready.
04 · Pinned with parallax layers

All three at once, at three depths.

Lines sit at different vertical positions and travel at different speeds as the user scrolls. Back layer drifts slowest + lightest, front layer moves fastest + sharpest. Reads as one slow-moving statement with depth — like text floating through a viewport.

Ship faster. Spend smarter. Stay audit-ready.

How to pick

  • V1 · Scrub reveal — safest, classiest, fits the existing brand pace. Most "ZopDev". 130–220vh total budget.
  • V2 · Word kinetic — biggest "wow" but heaviest motion + longest pin (280vh). Risk: viewer scrolls past before the build completes.
  • V3 · Morph — shortest budget (200vh) and biggest type. Reads like ad copy. Lets the brand statement own the whole viewport.
  • V4 · Parallax — most "designed". Looks more agency than product. Distinctive, but might fight the rest of the homepage's restraint.

My read

  • For shipping to index.html right now: V1 with a touch of V2's accent-color trick on the verbs. Keeps the existing pace, adds the brand orange as a load-bearing rhythm.
  • If the goal is the homepage hero moment (the thing people screenshot): V3.
  • If we want a portfolio piece: V2.
